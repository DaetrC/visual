Section 01
The World
What you need to understand before you touch anything.
This is not a music campaign. This is a world. The visual director's job is to make the world feel real before anyone hears a single note.
The Central Tension
A man who spent his life performing realizes he has become disconnected from the innocence that once made him who he was. Everything in this world moves people away from performance and toward authenticity.
The Enemy
Performance. Over authenticity. Over faith. Over identity. Over truth. Every visual choice should quietly resist performance.
The Feeling
The audience should feel like they discovered something, not that something was shown to them. The visual world should feel found, not produced.
What this world is not
A music video campaign · A rollout strategy · A brand refresh · A content calendar
What this world is
A living universe · A recovered archive · A spiritual document · A self-portrait in red

Section 04
Do / Don't
The guardrails. Non-negotiable on both pages.
Do
  • +Shoot in real environments, not sets
  • +Let negative space carry weight
  • +Use natural and practical lighting
  • +Let objects tell stories, not copy
  • +Every frame intentional and still
  • +Shoot moments, not poses
  • +Wardrobe matches the world always
  • +Treat the archive like evidence
  • +Honor visual separation between pages
  • +Let texture do emotional work on nNocent
Don't
  • -Trend-chase. This world has its own grammar.
  • -Over-produce the nNocent page
  • -Mix color palettes between pages
  • -Stage anything that should feel real
  • -Add unnecessary motion or filters
  • -Show nNocent's face on the archive page
  • -Let @daetrius feel like a music rollout
  • -Let @areyounNocent feel like a brand page
  • -Use luxury as a signifier
  • -Perform depth. Either it's there or it isn't.

Section 06
Reflections Visual Identity
The @daetrius content format. How real moments become the record.
The reflection is not a talking head video. It is a window into how a person thinks. The camera belongs in the room because it belongs there.
Reference Frames — Stefon Diggs Format
What These Frames Communicate
+
Intimate close-up framing. Face fills the frame. Mid-thought. No eye contact with camera.
+
Subject caught mid-thought. Not starting a sentence for the camera, finishing one for themselves.
+
Real environments as the story. Car, plane, outdoor, restaurant. Location tells you who the person is becoming.
+
Subtitle text as a visual element. Styled, weighted, intentional.
+
Low angles and POV shots. Puts the viewer inside the moment.
Subtitle Treatment
The question isn't what you sell.
It's what people become.
Gold / warm yellow · weighted sans · centered · max two lines
Shot Language — The Six Frames
The Close
Face fills 70%+ of frame. Mid-thought. No eye contact.
The Environment
Wide. Subject small. The room is the story.
The POV
Low angle. Handheld. Viewer feels present.
The Window
Subject framed through an opening. Two worlds in one frame.
The Hands
Gesture mid-speech. Hands as a secondary character.
The Still
Subject not speaking. Just existing. These frames are gold.
Approved Shoot Locations
Every location should feel like somewhere Daetrius actually is, not somewhere he was placed. The room is already his.

Archive · Confidential
@areyoun
Nocent
A 2006/2007 camera roll dropped into the present.
This page is not an artist page. It is not a promotion. It is a recovered archive. Evidence of a person who existed before the world told them who to be. The audience encounters fragments. They assemble meaning themselves. No explanation is ever provided.
The Lore
2006
nNocent has always existed. Before the operator. Before the strategy. Before the world. There was a kid in Atlanta performing in red hats with Nnocent Keep God First written across his chest. A kid rapper who signed a production deal at 12, opened for major artists, and believed completely that what he was doing mattered.

That kid did not drift. The world drifted around him. Performance crept in. The innocence did not disappear. It went underground.

The @areyounNocent archive is proof that it was always there. The audience is not watching a reinvention. They are watching a return.
NOW
Present-day nNocent moves through fly cars, gospel music on the stereo, no face visible. Vibing. Worshipping. Not performing for anyone. The archive captures both ends of the timeline. The two timelines coexist on the same page without explanation. The audience feels the gap before they understand it.
Visual Reference — Outfit Direction
Red-world aesthetic. No face required. Wide-leg silhouettes. Red on red. Graphic lettering. The clothes communicate the era and the world.
What this communicates
Wide-leg silhouettes. Red on red. No face. The clothes are the character. The uniform of someone who dressed for God, not for approval.
Archive Reference — nNocent's Studio
A primary content location for @areyounNocent. Shoot close. Shoot slow. Let the room breathe. Every object is an artifact.
Archive Reference — Lil nNocent
The youth footage. Low resolution. Real. The red hat, the Nnocent shirt, the microphone in hand. Drop it without context. Let the audience do the work.
Intentional Meme Direction
The meme is a content type, not a joke format. It carries the world's philosophy inside something the internet is already fluent in.
Content Types — What Goes On This Page
Archive Footage
Youth performances, old events, childhood recordings. Low res. No edit. No caption. Let the grain do the talking.
Gospel Ride-Alongs
Present-day nNocent in fly cars, stretch hummers, limousines. No face. Vibey gospel music. Worshipping in motion.
Unreleased Fragments
Snippets of music not yet in the world. 10 to 20 seconds. No context. No tracklist. Just the sound.
No-Face Outfits
Full red-world looks. Head cut off or turned. The clothes are the character.
Studio POV
Inside the studio. Objects, not faces. Evidence of a person at work. Close-up details only.
Intentional Memes
Cultural artifacts carrying philosophical weight. Faith-coded. Atlanta-coded. Never hollow.
Limo / Hummer Interiors
Inside luxury vehicles. Red interior or red-lit. Gospel playing. No destination shown.
Object Fragments
Close-up of a single object from the studio or from memory. No explanation. Just the thing, in light, in red.
The @areyounNocent page should feel like you found someone's phone from 2007 and you're scrolling through it. You do not know who it belongs to. But you recognize something in it.
